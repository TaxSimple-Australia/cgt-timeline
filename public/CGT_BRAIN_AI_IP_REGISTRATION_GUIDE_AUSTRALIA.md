## 2. Copyright Protection in Australia

### 2.1 Overview

**IMPORTANT: Copyright protection in Australia is FREE and AUTOMATIC.**

Unlike patents and trademarks, there is **no registration system** for copyright in Australia. Copyright protection arises automatically the moment an original work is created and expressed in a tangible form.

### 2.2 What Copyright Protects for Software

Copyright protects the following aspects of CGT Brain AI:

- **Source code** - All programming code (TypeScript, JavaScript, CSS, etc.)
- **Software documentation** - User manuals, technical specifications
- **User interface designs** - Original visual layouts and artistic elements
- **Database structures** - Original compilations of data
- **Literary works** - Any written content within the application
- **Sound recordings** - If the AI uses voice features

### 2.3 Duration of Copyright Protection

| Type of Work | Duration |
|-------------|----------|
| Computer programs (software) | 70 years from the end of the year the author dies |
| Literary works | 70 years from the end of the year the author dies |
| Sound recordings | 70 years from the end of the year first published |
| Films/Cinematographic works | 70 years from the end of the year first published |

### 2.4 How to Establish Copyright Ownership

While registration is not required, the following practices help prove ownership:

1. **Date stamp your work** - Maintain version control records (Git commits serve this purpose)
2. **Keep development records** - Preserve drafts, iterations, and development logs
3. **Include copyright notices** - Add notices to your code and materials:
   ```
   Â© 2024-2026 Tax Simple Australia Pty Ltd. All rights reserved.
   ```
4. **Use NDA agreements** - When sharing confidential information
5. **Employment contracts** - Ensure clear IP assignment clauses for developers
6. **Backup securely** - Maintain dated backups of all source code

### 2.5 Copyright Infringement and Enforcement

Copyright owners have exclusive rights to:
- Reproduce the work
- Publish the work
- Communicate the work to the public
- Make adaptations
- License these rights to others

**Enforcement options include:**
- Cease and desist letters
- Mediation and negotiation
- Court action for damages and injunctions
- Border protection (for physical goods)

### 2.6 Relevant Legislation

- **Copyright Act 1968 (Cth)** - Primary legislation
- **Copyright Regulations 2017** - Supplementary regulations
- **Copyright (International Protection) Regulations 1969**

## 3. Patent Registration in Australia

### 3.1 Overview

Patents protect **inventions** - new ways of doing things, technical solutions to problems, and innovations in how things work. For CGT Brain AI, patents could protect novel algorithms, technical processes, or innovative computational methods.

**Administered by:** IP Australia
**Website:** [https://www.ipaustralia.gov.au/patents](https://www.ipaustralia.gov.au/patents)

### 3.2 Types of Patents in Australia

| Patent Type | Protection Period | Status |
|------------|------------------|--------|
| **Standard Patent** | Up to 20 years (25 years for pharmaceuticals) | Available |
| **Provisional Patent** | 12 months (priority date holder) | Available |
| **Innovation Patent** | Was 8 years | **No longer available for new applications** |

### 3.3 Software Patent Eligibility in Australia

**Key 2025 Development - Aristocrat Decision:**

Following the landmark case *Aristocrat Technologies Australia Pty Ltd v Commissioner of Patents* [2025] FCAFC 131, the patentability of computer-implemented inventions (CIIs) in Australia has become more favourable.

**The test for patentability:**
- The invention must involve more than just manipulation of an abstract idea in a computer
- It must implement an abstract idea in a computer to produce an "artificial state of affairs and a useful result"
- The invention must have "technical character" in substance

**What CAN be patented:**
- Novel algorithms with technical application
- AI systems that solve technical problems
- Data processing methods that produce tangible results
- Software that improves computer functionality

**What CANNOT be patented:**
- Abstract ideas or mathematical formulas alone
- Business methods without technical implementation
- Mere presentations of information
- Schemes or plans without technical substance

### 3.4 Patent Application Process

#### Step-by-Step Process:

**Step 1: Research Your Invention**
- Keep your invention confidential before filing
- Decide between provisional, standard, or international (PCT) patent
- Research costs and renewal fees

**Step 2: Search Existing Patents**
- Use AusPat database: [https://inspire.wipo.int/auspat](https://inspire.wipo.int/auspat)
- Search international databases
- Check for prior art

**Step 3: Prepare Required Documents**
- Ownership and inventor details
- Australian/New Zealand agent address
- Detailed specification (description and claims)
- Payment information
- Sequence listings (if applicable)

**Step 4: File Your Application**
- Create account with IP Australia Online Services
- Complete application form (approximately 15 minutes)
- Upload specification document
- Pay application fee

**Step 5: Publication**
- Patent published in Australian Official Journal of Patents
- Available in Australian Patent Search database

**Step 6: Request Examination**
- Can be requested at filing or within 5 years
- Pay examination fee
- Processing typically takes up to 12 months

**Step 7: Address Examination Issues**
- 12 months to resolve any issues raised
- Failure to resolve causes application to lapse

**Step 8: Acceptance and Grant**
- If approved, published for 3-month opposition period
- Third parties can oppose grant
- Once granted, protection begins

### 3.5 Provisional Patent Applications

A provisional patent is an **optional, inexpensive first step** that:
- Establishes your priority date
- Gives you 12 months to decide on full patent
- Signals to competitors you're seeking protection
- Keeps technical details confidential
- Enables international-type search option

**Important:** A provisional patent does NOT provide enforceable protection. You must convert to a standard patent within 12 months.

### 3.6 International Patent Protection (PCT)

The **Patent Cooperation Treaty (PCT)** allows filing a single international application for protection in 150+ countries.

**Key features:**
- Single application for multiple countries
- One set of formalities
- No immediate translation required
- 31 months from priority date to enter national phases

**Filing options:**
- ePCT (WIPO's online system): [https://www.wipo.int/pct/en/](https://www.wipo.int/pct/en/)
- Through IP Australia as Receiving Office

**IP Australia's role:**
- Acts as Receiving Office (RO)
- International Searching Authority (ISA)
- International Preliminary Examining Authority (IPEA)

## 4. Trademark Registration in Australia

### 4.1 Overview

A trademark protects your **brand** - the name, logo, slogan, or other identifiers that distinguish your goods and services from competitors.

For CGT Brain AI, trademarks would protect:
- The name "CGT Brain AI"
- The company logo
- Any distinctive slogans or taglines
- Potentially distinctive UI elements

**Administered by:** IP Australia
**Website:** [https://www.ipaustralia.gov.au/trade-marks](https://www.ipaustralia.gov.au/trade-marks)

### 4.2 What Can Be Trademarked

**Registrable trademarks include:**
- Words and phrases (e.g., "CGT Brain AI")
- Logos and graphics
- Letters and numbers
- Sounds (jingles, distinctive sounds)
- Scents (in limited cases)
- Shapes (product packaging)
- Colours (if distinctive)
- Combinations of the above

**What CANNOT be trademarked:**
- Generic or descriptive terms
- Geographic names (unless distinctive)
- Common surnames
- Misleading or deceptive marks
- Scandalous or offensive marks
- Prohibited marks (national flags, restricted terms like "bank" or "university")

### 4.3 Trademark Classes for Software

Australia uses the **Nice Classification System** with 45 classes (1-34 for goods, 35-45 for services).

**Relevant classes for CGT Brain AI:**

| Class | Description | Relevance |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **Class 9** | Downloadable software, apps, computer hardware | Downloadable CGT Brain AI software |
| **Class 35** | Business services, advertising, data management | Business consulting, data analysis services |
| **Class 36** | Financial services, tax services | CGT calculation and tax-related services |
| **Class 42** | Scientific/technological services, SaaS, software development | Cloud-based CGT Brain AI platform, IT services |

**Note:** You should register in ALL classes relevant to your business. Software companies typically need both Class 9 (downloadable software) AND Class 42 (SaaS and development services).

### 4.4 Trademark Application Process

#### Step 1: Determine Ownership
- Legal owner must be specified (individual, company, trustee)
- Business names, trusts, and partnerships cannot directly own trademarks
- The legal entity (individual partners, trustees) must apply

#### Step 2: Check Eligibility
- Must reside in Australia/New Zealand OR have an agent there
- Must intend to use the trademark for specified goods/services

#### Step 3: Research Existing Trademarks
- Use **TM Checker** (free AI-powered tool): [https://www.ipaustralia.gov.au/trade-marks/search-existing-trade-marks/tm-checker](https://www.ipaustralia.gov.au/trade-marks/search-existing-trade-marks/tm-checker)
- Search **Australian Trade Mark Search**: [https://search.ipaustralia.gov.au/trademarks/search/quick](https://search.ipaustralia.gov.au/trademarks/search/quick)
- Check business name register (ASIC)

#### Step 4: Choose Application Type

**Option A: TM Headstart (Recommended for first-time filers)**
- Pre-application service with examiner feedback
- 5 business days to receive assessment
- 5 business days to make amendments
- Starting cost: $330 per class

**Option B: Standard Application**
- Direct filing with priority date
- Limited post-submission changes
- Starting cost: $250 per class

#### Step 5: Gather Documentation
- Legal name and contact information
- Trademark representation (image file or description)
- List of goods/services with class numbers
- Payment details

#### Step 6: File Online
- Create IP Australia Online Services account
- Complete application form (~15 minutes)
- Upload trademark image/representation
- Pay fees

#### Step 7: Examination
- Processing takes 3-4 months
- Examiner checks distinctiveness and conflicts
- May receive adverse report requiring response

#### Step 8: Acceptance and Publication
- If accepted, published in Australian Official Journal of Trade Marks
- 2-month opposition period
- Third parties can oppose registration

#### Step 9: Registration
- If no opposition (or opposition overcome), trademark registered
- Certificate of Registration issued
- Protection begins from filing date

### 4.5 TM Headstart Service Details

**Three-Step Process:**

**Step 1: Submit Request ($200 per class)**
- Create online account
- Submit trademark for assessment
- Within 5 business days, receive examiner's assessment report

**Step 2: Amendment (Optional)**
- 5 business days to make changes based on feedback
- Mark changes: $150
- Additional class: $200 per class

**Step 3: Formalize Application (Optional)**
- 5 business days to convert to formal application
- Conversion fee: $130 per class
- Filing date locks in

**Total minimum cost:** $330 for one class

**Benefits:**
- Feedback before formal filing
- Opportunity to correct errors
- Lower risk of rejection

**Limitations:**
- Cannot check for conflicting registrations
- Cannot guarantee success
- Cannot provide legal advice

## 5. Business Name / Trade Name Registration

### 5.1 Overview

A **business name** is the name you trade under, which may be different from your legal company name. In Australia, business names are registered with ASIC (Australian Securities and Investments Commission).

**IMPORTANT NOTE ON TRADING NAMES:**
As of **1 November 2025**, unregistered "trading names" were removed from public visibility on the Australian Business Register (ABR) and ABN Lookup. All businesses must now register their business name through ASIC.

**Administered by:** ASIC
**Website:** [https://www.asic.gov.au/for-business-and-companies/business-names/](https://www.asic.gov.au/for-business-and-companies/business-names/)

### 5.2 When Business Name Registration is Required

You **MUST** register a business name if you trade under a name different from:
- Your own first name and surname (for sole traders)
- All partners' personal names (for partnerships)
- Your registered company name (for companies)

You **DO NOT** need to register if:
- You're a sole trader using your exact first name and surname
- All partners in a partnership trade under their personal names
- You're a company trading under your exact registered company name

### 5.3 Business Name vs Trademark

| Feature | Business Name (ASIC) | Trademark (IP Australia) |
|---------|---------------------|-------------------------|
| Protection scope | Prevents identical/nearly identical names | Prevents similar marks causing confusion |
| Geographic scope | Australia-wide | Australia (can extend internationally) |
| Duration | 1 or 3 years (renewable) | 10 years (renewable indefinitely) |
| Legal protection | Limited (just prevents same name) | Strong (can sue for infringement) |
| Recommendation | Register both for comprehensive protection | Register both for comprehensive protection |

### 5.4 Requirements for Registration

**Prerequisites:**
- Must have an ABN (or have applied for one)
- ABN reference number if ABN is pending

**Information needed:**
- Proposed business name
- ABN (or application reference)
- Business address details
- Holder's details (individual or entity)

### 5.5 Registration Process

#### Step 1: Check Name Availability
- Search ASIC Business Names Register: [https://www.asic.gov.au/online-services/search-asic-registers/business-names-register/](https://www.asic.gov.au/online-services/search-asic-registers/business-names-register/)
- Use TM Checker to check for trademark conflicts: [https://www.ipaustralia.gov.au/trade-marks/search-existing-trade-marks/tm-checker](https://www.ipaustralia.gov.au/trade-marks/search-existing-trade-marks/tm-checker)

#### Step 2: Apply Through Business Registration Service
- Government's Business Registration Service: [https://register.business.gov.au/](https://register.business.gov.au/)
- OR ASIC's online registration system

#### Step 3: Choose Registration Period
- 1 year: $45
- 3 years: $104

#### Step 4: Pay and Complete
- Payment via credit/debit card
- Registration typically processed within 48 hours
- Certificate of registration provided

## 6. Company Name Registration

### 6.1 Overview

If operating as a company (Pty Ltd), the company must be registered with ASIC. The company name becomes a legal identifier and provides nationwide exclusivity.

**Administered by:** ASIC
**Website:** [https://www.asic.gov.au/for-business-and-companies/companies/register-a-company/](https://www.asic.gov.au/for-business-and-companies/companies/register-a-company/)

### 6.2 Company Registration Requirements

**Essential requirements:**
- Unique, available company name
- Physical registered office address in Australia
- At least one director who is an Australian resident
- At least one shareholder
- Director ID for all directors (mandatory before registration)
- Constitution (optional - can use replaceable rules)

**Director ID requirement:**
- All directors must obtain a Director ID before company registration
- Apply at: [https://www.abrs.gov.au/director-identification-number](https://www.abrs.gov.au/director-identification-number)

### 6.3 Company Types

| Type | Abbreviation | Description |
|------|--------------|-------------|
| Proprietary Limited | Pty Ltd | Private company (most common for businesses) |
| Proprietary Unlimited | Pty | Private company with unlimited liability |
| Public Limited | Ltd | Public company (can offer shares to public) |
| Public Unlimited | - | Public company with unlimited liability |
| No Liability | NL | Mining companies only |

### 6.4 Registration Process

#### Step 1: Check Name Availability
- ASIC Name Check: [https://www.asic.gov.au/online-services/search-asic-registers/](https://www.asic.gov.au/online-services/search-asic-registers/)

#### Step 2: Obtain Director IDs
- All directors must have Director ID before registration

#### Step 3: Prepare Documentation
- Proposed company name
- Registered office address
- Director details (names, DOB, residential addresses)
- Shareholder details
- Share structure

#### Step 4: Submit Application
- Business Registration Service: [https://register.business.gov.au/](https://register.business.gov.au/)
- Form 201: Application for Registration as an Australian Company

#### Step 5: Pay Fees and Complete
- Registration fee: approximately $611 (as of 1 July 2025)
- Processing: 2-5 business days
- Receive ACN (Australian Company Number)

### 6.5 Company Registration Fees (1 July 2025 - 30 June 2026)

- **Annual review:** Complete and pay fee each year
- **Update details:** Notify ASIC of changes within 28 days

### 7.3 Eligibility Requirements

**For .com.au and .net.au:**
- Australian sole trader, business, or company
- Valid ABN or ACN
- Domain must relate to your entity name, business name, or trademark
- OR have "close and substantial connection" to your goods/services

- Typically through ABN, ACN, or trademark registration

## 8. Design Rights Registration

### 8.1 Overview

Design rights protect the **visual appearance** of products - their shape, configuration, pattern, and ornamentation. For CGT Brain AI, this could protect distinctive visual UI elements if they're sufficiently novel and distinctive.

**Administered by:** IP Australia
**Website:** [https://www.ipaustralia.gov.au/design-rights](https://www.ipaustralia.gov.au/design-rights)

### 8.2 What Design Rights Protect

Design rights protect the **visual appearance** of products, including:
- Shape and configuration
- Pattern and ornamentation
- The overall visual impression

**For software applications:**
- Distinctive icon designs
- Unique UI layouts (if applied to a product)
- Visual representations of data
- Physical product packaging

**Note:** Design rights protect appearance, not function. For functional aspects, patents are required.

### 8.3 Two-Part Process

Design registration in Australia has **two parts**:

**Part 1: Registration**
- Establishes ownership record
- Publishes design publicly
- Does NOT provide enforceable rights

**Part 2: Certification**
- Examines novelty and distinctiveness
- Provides enforceable legal rights
- Required before taking legal action

### 8.4 Registration Process

#### Part 1: Registration

**Step 1: Lodge Application**
- Online through IP Australia
- Provide ownership and contact details
- Upload design representation (photo/drawing)
- Specify product name
- Pay application fee

**Step 2: Publication**
- Details published about application

**Step 3: Formalities Assessment (8 weeks)**
- IP Australia checks legislative compliance

**Step 4: Address Issues**
- 2 months to resolve any problems

**Step 5: Registration**
- Certificate of Registration issued
- Valid for 5 years
- Design appears in Australian Design Search

#### Part 2: Certification

**Step 1: Request Examination**
- Can be requested at filing or later
- Pay examination fee

**Step 2: Examination (13 weeks)**
- Global search for similar designs
- Assessment of novelty and distinctiveness

**Step 3: Resolve Objections**
- 6 months to address any issues

**Step 4: Certification**
- Certificate of Examination issued
- Enforceable legal rights granted

### 8.5 Design Rights Fees (From October 2024)

| Service | Fee (AUD) |
|---------|-----------|
| New single design application | $200 |
| Additional design (same Locarno class) | $150 |
| Excess design during formalities | $200 |
| Examination request (owner) | $500 |
| Registered owner fee | $250 |

**Attorney fees:** Typically $700 - $1,200 for filing

### 8.6 Protection Duration

| Period | Status |
|--------|--------|
| Initial term | 5 years from filing date |
| Renewal | One additional 5-year term |
| Maximum protection | 10 years total |
| Renewal timing | Before expiry of first 5 years |
| Grace period | 6 months (with surcharges) |
